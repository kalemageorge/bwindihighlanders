Uganda is recognized as having a complex land tenure system, with four (4) land tenure systems including freehold, leasehold, private mailo, customary freehold and crown land, all sometimes existing within the vicinity of each other. In addition, land disputes in families after owners are deceased have split many families apart even after the deceased clearly indicated who is to inherit the given piece of land. This is mainly because evidence and power to dictate who will own the land is no longer in the hands of the given individual. Also, wealthy people have a tendency to forcefully own land for the less advantaged people like the poor, disabled or orphans because there are no clear ways to prove it's their land.
